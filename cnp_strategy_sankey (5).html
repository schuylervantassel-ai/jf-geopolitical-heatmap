◈ FRAMEWORK DEFINITION
COMPREHENSIVE NATIONAL POWER (综合国力)
A systems-theory model measuring state power as a weighted composite of military strength, economic output, S&T capacity, political cohesion, and cultural influence. First formalised by Chinese strategists in 1984.

The framework defines national power as a non-linear complex system: small imbalances in any sub-index cause disproportionately large distortions in total output. Balance is not optional — it is a structural imperative.

What drives PRC foreign policy is not absolute CNP level but rank relative to rivals — particularly the narrowing gap between PRC and US CNP scores. Part III shows that every major foreign policy shift from 1993 to 2020 correlates directly with a change in the comparative CNP assessment, not with leadership personality.
